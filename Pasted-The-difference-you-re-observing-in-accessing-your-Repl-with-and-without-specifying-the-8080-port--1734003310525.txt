The difference you're observing in accessing your Repl (with and without specifying the :8080 port) likely arises from changes in Replit's networking configuration or how your application is listening for incoming connections. Let me explain the situation and steps to clarify and potentially resolve this.

Why Did the Behavior Change?
Default Port Handling:

Previously, your app might have been defaulting to port 80, the standard HTTP port. Browsers automatically assume :80 when a port is not explicitly specified.
Now, the app is explicitly listening on port 8080, requiring you to include the port in the URL (:8080).
Server Port Binding:

The server might now be explicitly binding to 8080 instead of 80 or not correctly detecting the environment variable PORT provided by Replit.
Replit Platform Updates:

Replit may have changed its handling of ports, especially if you're using multiple ports in your configuration (e.g., 3000, 5000, 8080).
This could result in the requirement to specify the port explicitly for certain Repls.
